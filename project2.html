Amazon Book Reviews Sentiment Analysis

Using machine learning to classify sentiment in Amazon book reviews and understand patterns in customer feedback.

Overview

This project analyzes Amazon book reviews to extract customer sentiment and identify patterns in user feedback. Using a large-scale reviews dataset from Kaggle, the goal was to build a model that predicts whether a review is positive or negative and provide insights that can support product and marketing decisions.

Data & Tools

Worked with the Amazon Books Reviews dataset, which spans nearly two decades of customer reviews and ratings across hundreds of thousands of book titles. For modeling, a smaller, balanced subset was sampled and manually labeled.

Tools: Python, Pandas, scikit-learn, VADER, Naive Bayes, SVM, Excel

Approach

  • Sampled 2,000 reviews from the full dataset and curated a balanced labeled subset of 500 reviews.
  • Cleaned and preprocessed review text, removed duplicates, and standardized formatting.
  • Compared sentiment labeling methods including VADER, Logistic Regression, and Naive Bayes.
  • Used manually labeled data to train and evaluate multiple classifiers (Decision Tree, Random Forest, SVM).

Results & Impact

  • Support Vector Machine achieved the best overall performance, with accuracy around 86% on the labeled set.
  • Balanced recall across sentiment classes reduced bias toward a single label.
  • Demonstrated a workflow that can be scaled to monitor customer sentiment at scale and help businesses understand perception of products, refine marketing strategies, and improve customer experience.
