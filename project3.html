“THIS IS MUSIC” – Music Industry SQL Analytics

Building a relational database for the music industry and using SQL to analyze artist performance, revenue streams, awards, and long-term career patterns.

Overview

This project designs a database management system (DBMS) for the music industry and uses SQL to study how artist influence, earnings, and awards can inform business decisions for record labels, investors, and marketing teams. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Data & Schema

  • Source: Multiple Kaggle datasets covering Grammy awards, albums, revenue, and richest musicians. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • Core tables: GRAMMYAWARDS, ALBUM, MONEYMAKER, RICHESTMUSICIAN, RECORD.
  • Designed ER diagram and 3rd Normal Form relational schema to capture artists, awards, earnings, and recordings. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Tools: SQL (MySQL / MariaDB), ER modeling, relational design.

Analytic Focus

  • Identify artists with high commercial and award success.
  • Evaluate which revenue streams (sales, streaming, publishing, touring) drive total profit. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  • Understand how awards, longevity, and genre relate to financial outcomes.

Business Insights – Artists & Revenue

  • Ranked artists by total earnings and Grammy wins to help record labels and talent agencies prioritize which artists to sign or invest in. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
  • Broke down income into sales, streaming, publishing, and touring to reveal each artist’s strongest revenue channels and optimization opportunities. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
  • Provided insights useful for record labels, investors, and private equity firms evaluating music catalogs.

Business Insights – Awards, Longevity & Genre

  • Compared earnings between Grammy-winning and non-winning artists to test whether awards correlate with higher financial success. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
  • Calculated career length using first and last recorded years to study artist longevity and total earnings over time. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
  • Counted Grammy wins by genre to identify which genres generate the most traction and can guide signing or collaboration strategies. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Future Opportunities

  • Extend the DBMS with regional listening and touring data to support tour planning and market expansion decisions (e.g., Asia). :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}
  • Integrate sponsorship and brand-deal data to match artists with ideal brand partners. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
  • Use the existing schema as a foundation for dashboards for A&R teams and music investors.
